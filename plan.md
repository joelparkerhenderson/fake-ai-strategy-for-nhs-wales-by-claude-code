## 2. Critical success factors

These are the conditions without which the plan cannot succeed. The AI Council will review them quarterly:

1. **Welsh Government commitment** to a 3-year ring-fenced AI funding envelope (~£100–£150m).
2. **A single accountable senior responsible owner (SRO)** per workstream, with named deputies.
3. **Clinician partnership from day 1** — co-design, not consultation.
4. **An empowered DHCW** with the legal mandate, resource, and senior cover to act as the national AI delivery vehicle.
5. **A bilingual-by-default operating principle** policed at procurement.
6. **Public trust earned by transparency** — the AI Register is the load-bearing artefact.
7. **Honest evaluation** — falsification signals (§14.4 of strategy) lead to pause-and-replan.

### 6.3 Stage-gate decisions during Phase 2

At the end of each quarter the AI Council will hold a **stage-gate review** for each in-flight programme, using the One London six-stage lifecycle: Idea → PoC → Pilot → Evaluate → Scale → Monitor.[^onelondon] The review will result in one of four decisions:

- **Proceed** to next stage.
- **Hold** — outstanding actions before next stage.
- **Pause** — concerns require investigation.
- **Stop** — programme value or safety not evidenced; decommission.

Mandatory stop-decision triggers:

- Bias audit failure not remediable within 90 days;
- Material clinical safety incident attributable to the AI;
- Failure to meet bilingual standard at deployment;
- Realised benefit < 30% of business case at 12 months post-Scale.

## 12. Issues management

Issues are risks that have materialised. Each issue raised will be triaged within 5 working days by the AI Delivery Board:

- **Severity 1**: clinical safety, public trust, data breach. Escalate to AI Council and Cabinet Secretary same day.
- **Severity 2**: programme delay >1 quarter or >£5m budget impact. Reported next AI Council.
- **Severity 3**: operational. Resolved at workstream level.

A standing **issues log** will be maintained by the AI Council secretariat and published in summary form in the quarterly public report.

## 16. How this plan will change

This plan is intended to be **a living document**, refreshed at:

- **Each quarterly AI Council meeting** (operational adjustments).
- **Each annual report cycle** (re-baselined milestones, revised KPI targets if evidenced).
- **A formal mid-cycle review in 2028** that will produce a Plan v2 for 2029–2031, informed by all available evaluation evidence.

Material changes will be:

- Recorded in the version history of this document;
- Briefed to the Welsh Government Cabinet Secretary;
- Reflected in the next public report.
