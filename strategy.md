## 1. Foreword

NHS Wales serves a population of approximately 3.1 million people across seven Local Health Boards (Aneurin Bevan, Betsi Cadwaladr, Cardiff and Vale, Cwm Taf Morgannwg, Hywel Dda, Powys and Swansea Bay), three NHS Trusts (Welsh Ambulance Services NHS Trust, Velindre University NHS Trust and Public Health Wales) and two special health authorities (Digital Health and Care Wales — DHCW — and Health Education and Improvement Wales — HEIW). Together we employ around 100,000 people and steward an annual budget in excess of £11 billion.[^nhswales-overview]

Artificial Intelligence is no longer a horizon technology. It is reading our stroke scans today, in every stroke unit in England, with similar deployment now underway across Wales.[^lordslib] It is summarising consultations, predicting attendance, optimising rotas and supporting research at a pace and scale that demands a deliberate, ethical and uniquely *Welsh* response.

This strategy is that response. It is grounded in *Cymru Iachach / A Healthier Wales*[^healthier-wales] and the Welsh Government's *Digital and data strategy for health and social care in Wales*.[^wg-digital] It builds on the work already underway through DHCW's National Data Resource, HEIW's *Building AI Skills in NHS Wales* programme[^heiw-aiskills] and the AI Landscape Review,[^heiw-landscape] and aligns with UK-wide instruments including the *Artificial Intelligence Playbook for the UK Government*[^uk-playbook] and NHS England's regulatory and ethical guidance.[^nhse-ai]

We make three commitments. First, **safety and trust come before speed**: every clinical AI tool deployed in NHS Wales will carry meaningful human oversight, will be registered on a public AI register, and will be audited for bias on a published cadence. Second, **the Welsh language is a first-class citizen**: AI tools that touch patients, citizens or staff will be available bilingually from day one of deployment, in line with the *Welsh Language (Wales) Measure 2011*[^welsh-measure] and the *More than just words / Mwy na geiriau* framework.[^morethanjustwords] Third, **the benefits will be shared**: we will design once and deploy seven times, so that a citizen in rural Powys receives the same AI-augmented care as a citizen in central Cardiff.

— *The fictional Cabinet Secretary for Health and Social Care, Welsh Government*, and *the fictional Chief Executive, NHS Wales*.

## 2. Executive summary

NHS Wales has a narrow window to seize the productivity, quality-of-care and patient-experience gains of artificial intelligence — and an equally narrow window to do so on terms that protect the values of our health service: equity, bilingualism, public ownership and clinician primacy.

This strategy sets a five-year direction (2026–2031) with a focused three-year delivery plan (2026–2028).

**Vision.** *To make NHS Wales the first nation in the United Kingdom to deploy safe, ethical, bilingual artificial intelligence at scale across an integrated public health and social care system, measurably improving outcomes for the people of Wales while protecting the values of a publicly-owned NHS.*

**Three strategic priorities.**

1. **Enhance clinical decision-making and care** through AI-assisted diagnostics, predictive deterioration, ambient documentation and personalised pathways.
2. **Streamline operations and improve flow** through AI-powered scheduling, predictive bed and ICU management, theatre optimisation and DNA reduction.
3. **Optimise non-clinical workflows** through generative AI in correspondence, RTT pathway closure, FOI responses, procurement and corporate functions.

**Four strategic enablers.**

1. **Infrastructure, data and bilingual AI capability** — National Data Resource, sovereign compute, Welsh-language model layer.
2. **Governance, ethics and trust** — National AI Safety Council; AI Register; FUTURE-AI compliance;[^future-ai] alignment with the UK AI Playbook.
3. **Workforce, skills and culture** — partnership with HEIW; AI Literacy 101 for all staff; AI-augmented practice for clinicians; Welsh-language AI fluency.
4. **Partnership, research and innovation** — National Health AI Sandbox; collaboration with Welsh universities (Cardiff, Swansea, Bangor, Aberystwyth) and the four UK nations.

**Five SMART OKRs** are set for 2026–2028, with 25 measurable Key Results and a 32-indicator KPI framework summarised in §13.

**Estimated three-year benefit envelope** (illustrative, see §14): **£300–£480 million** of value created across NHS Wales (workforce time, avoided length of stay, reduced diagnostic backlogs, fewer DNAs, fraud and waste reduction), against a phased investment of approximately **£95–£135 million**. **Net benefit of £180–£350 million** at a benefit–cost ratio (BCR) of approximately **2.4–3.6×**, with payback achieved during Year 2.

**Ten flagship initiatives** are committed for delivery by end-2028, ranging from a pan-Wales AI stroke imaging platform to a Welsh-language clinical-summarisation LLM service.

## 3. Introduction and scope

### 3.1 Definition of AI used in this strategy

We adopt the working definition used in the One London framework: *AI is an umbrella term for a range of algorithm-based technologies that solve complex tasks by carrying out functions that previously required human thinking.*[^onelondon] We include the following technology classes, in order of increasing complexity:

| Class | Examples |
|---|---|
| Decision trees | Rules-based clinical decision support |
| Robotic Process Automation (RPA) | RTT pathway closure, OCR for paper records |
| Advanced statistical modelling | Bayesian regression for risk stratification |
| Classical Machine Learning | XGBoost, Random Forest for risk prediction |
| Deep Learning | CNNs for radiology, RNNs for time-series |
| Generative AI / Foundation Models | LLMs for ambient scribing, summarisation, Q&A |

The strategy distinguishes between **AI as a Medical Device (AIaMD)**, which falls within the scope of the *Medical Device Regulations 2002* and MHRA oversight, and **AI for non-clinical use** (administration, research, operations), which is governed primarily by data protection, employment and equality law.[^onelondon]

### 3.2 Scope of this strategy

This strategy applies to **all NHS Wales statutory bodies** — the seven Health Boards, three Trusts and two Special Health Authorities — and to all third-party suppliers processing NHS Wales data. It is supported by, and does not supersede:

- DHCW *Information & Analytics Strategy 2023–2026*;[^dhcw-ia]
- HEIW *Digital Workforce Strategy* and *Building AI Skills in NHS Wales*;[^heiw-aiskills]
- Welsh Government *Digital and data strategy for health and social care in Wales*;[^wg-digital]
- *A Healthier Wales / Cymru Iachach* long-term plan;[^healthier-wales]
- The relevant statutory instruments in §11.

### 3.3 Period covered

- **Strategic horizon**: 2026–2031 (five years, aligned to the next iteration of *A Healthier Wales*).
- **Delivery plan horizon**: 2026–2028 (three years).
- **Annual refresh**: every March, with a comprehensive mid-cycle review in 2028.

## 4. Vision, mission and values

### 4.1 Vision

> *To make NHS Wales the first nation in the United Kingdom to deploy safe, ethical, bilingual artificial intelligence at scale across an integrated public health and social care system, measurably improving outcomes for the people of Wales while protecting the values of a publicly-owned NHS.*

### 4.2 Mission

We will use AI to:

1. **Give time back to clinicians** so they can spend more of it with patients.
2. **Diagnose earlier and more accurately**, especially for cancer, stroke, cardiovascular disease and dementia.
3. **Reduce inequities** in access, experience and outcomes — including the rural–urban, deprivation, and Welsh–English language gaps.
4. **Make NHS Wales financially and environmentally sustainable** by reducing avoidable demand, waste and unnecessary energy consumption.
5. **Earn and retain public trust** through radical transparency about how, where, when and by whom AI is used.

### 4.3 Values — *Y Gwerthoedd*

Our six values are derived from the *Welsh NHS Core Values*,[^nhswales-values] the *FUTURE-AI* international consensus,[^future-ai] and the UK AI Playbook:[^uk-playbook]

| Welsh value | English | What it means for AI |
|---|---|---|
| *Gofalu* | Care | AI must improve, not erode, the human relationship at the heart of care. |
| *Tegwch* | Fairness / Equity | AI must reduce, not entrench, health inequalities — including for Welsh speakers. |
| *Tryloywder* | Transparency | The public has a right to know where AI is used in their care. |
| *Diogelwch* | Safety | Human clinicians retain decision-making authority. AI augments; it does not replace. |
| *Cynaliadwyedd* | Sustainability | Compute, data and supply chains must align with NHS Wales' net-zero ambition (2030/2040).[^nhswales-netzero] |
| *Cydweithio* | Collaboration | We design, build, evaluate and scale together — across Health Boards, the four nations, academia and citizens. |

## 5. The case for change in NHS Wales

### 5.1 The pressures

NHS Wales is operating under sustained pressure that AI is uniquely well-placed to mitigate, though never alone:

- **Workforce constraint.** Vacancy rates across nursing, allied health and primary care medicine remain elevated; HEIW's workforce planning identifies persistent gaps to 2030.[^heiw-workforce]
- **Demand growth.** An ageing population (24% of Wales is aged 65+, the highest proportion in the UK) and rising multimorbidity drive year-on-year activity growth that has historically outstripped capacity growth.[^ons-wales]
- **Waiting times.** Despite recent recovery progress, the elective backlog remains a defining political and clinical challenge of the Senedd term.[^stats-wales-rtt]
- **Health inequalities.** Gaps in healthy life expectancy between the most and least deprived deciles remain stark and have not narrowed materially since 2015.[^phw-inequality]
- **Financial sustainability.** Health Boards face recurring deficits; productivity growth in the UK NHS has lagged the wider economy for over a decade.[^health-foundation]
- **Climate emergency.** The NHS in Wales is statutorily required to contribute to net zero, and has set ambitious 2030 (own emissions) and 2040 (full footprint) targets.[^nhswales-netzero]

### 5.2 The opportunity

External evidence — much of it now empirically validated, not merely projected — suggests AI can address several of these pressures simultaneously:

- **Diagnostic acceleration.** Thanks to the AI Diagnostic Fund, *50% of hospital trusts in England are now deploying AI to help diagnose conditions like lung cancer, with hospitals using AI-supported diagnostics seeing a 42% reduction in diagnostic errors*.[^lordslib]
- **Stroke care.** *AI is already being used to analyse and interpret acute stroke brain scans to support doctors when diagnosing and making treatment decisions in 100% of stroke units in England*.[^lordslib]
- **Ambient documentation.** GOSH's participation in the London-wide TORTUS ambient AI pilot in 2024 demonstrated *an average 10% reduction in consultation time per appointment*.[^gosh] At NHS-Wales scale (~5 million outpatient appointments per year) this is materially equivalent to thousands of clinician days released annually.
- **Generative AI for administration.** A 2024 Buckinghamshire Council pilot showed *administrators averaging 60–90 minutes saved per day*, with overall efficiency gains of 10–20%.[^gosh]

The lesson is not that AI is a silver bullet — it is not — but that the productivity and quality dividend is now real, measured, and accruing to systems that have moved decisively. NHS Wales must be one of those systems, on its own terms.

### 5.3 The risks of *not* acting

Inaction is not a neutral option. The risks of a "wait and see" posture include:

- **Vendor lock-in by default**, as commercial AI tools enter through unmanaged "shadow IT" without governance.
- **Widening inequities** as more affluent patients access privately-procured AI services that NHS Wales does not commission for all.
- **Clinician burnout and attrition** as English NHS trusts deploy ambient documentation and Welsh trusts do not.
- **Erosion of the Welsh language in healthcare**, as global LLMs default to English-only interactions.
- **Loss of research leadership** as AI-ready data infrastructure becomes a precondition for funding and trial siting.

## 6. Strategic priorities

We will deliver this vision through three priority areas, each underpinned by four strategic enablers (§7).

### 6.1 Priority 1 — Enhance clinical decision-making and care

**Rationale.** This is where AI's clinical evidence base is strongest and where patient benefit is most direct.

**Programmes.**

| # | Programme | Description | Lead | Years |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1.1 | **National AI Imaging Platform** | Pan-Wales deployment of MHRA-approved AI for CT, MRI, X-ray and digital pathology, starting with stroke (RAPID/Brainomix-class), lung cancer screening, and chest X-ray triage. | DHCW + Velindre + WAST | 2026–2028 |
| 1.2 | **Predictive deterioration in acute care** | NEWS2-augmented ML for early warning in inpatient and ED settings, including paediatric (PEWS) variants. | Health Boards | 2026–2027 |
| 1.3 | **Cancer pathway acceleration** | AI for histopathology slide review, faecal-immunochemical-test triage, dermatology teledermoscopy, and AI-prioritised cancer multi-disciplinary team (MDT) preparation. | Velindre + Health Boards | 2027–2028 |
| 1.4 | **Personalised medicine and genomics** | Integration of the All Wales Medical Genomics Service (AWMGS) with AI-driven variant interpretation for rare disease and cancer. | AWMGS + DHCW | 2027–2029 |
| 1.5 | **Ambient AI clinical documentation** | National framework agreement and rollout of ambient scribing across primary care, outpatients and ED, in Welsh and English. | DHCW + Primary Care | 2026–2028 |

### 6.2 Priority 2 — Streamline operations, reduce waiting times and improve flow

**Rationale.** Operational AI delivers the fastest payback, the lowest clinical risk and is the most credible source of recurrent funding for Priority 1 expansion.

**Programmes.**

| # | Programme | Description | Lead | Years |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2.1 | **Predictive ED and bed management** | ML-driven 4-hour, 12-hour and bed occupancy forecasts; early warning for capacity escalation. | Health Boards + WAST | 2026–2027 |
| 2.2 | **Theatre and outpatient scheduling** | Optimisation models for surgical lists, outpatient slot utilisation and pooled list management. | Health Boards | 2026–2028 |
| 2.3 | **Did Not Attend (DNA) reduction** | Patient-level DNA risk prediction with tailored reminders; bilingual SMS and chatbot follow-up. | DHCW + Health Boards | 2026–2027 |
| 2.4 | **Ambulance triage and dispatch** | AI-augmented call handling, clinical-need prediction and category-validation. | WAST | 2027–2028 |
| 2.5 | **RTT pathway intelligence** | RPA + ML for pathway closure when patients return to GP; predictive backlog clearance modelling. | DHCW + Health Boards | 2026–2027 |

### 6.3 Priority 3 — Optimise non-clinical, corporate and population-health workflows

**Rationale.** Generative and conventional AI in back-office functions delivers immediate productivity gains, frees money for the front line, and is a low-risk training ground for the workforce.

**Programmes.**

| # | Programme | Description | Lead | Years |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 3.1 | **NHS Wales Copilot programme** | Tenanted, NHS-Wales-only generative AI service (Microsoft Copilot for M365 / Google equivalent / sovereign alternative) with bilingual support. | DHCW | 2026–2027 |
| 3.2 | **Discharge summary and correspondence assistance** | LLM-assisted drafting of discharge summaries, clinic letters, FOI responses, with mandatory clinician sign-off. | Health Boards | 2026–2028 |
| 3.3 | **Procurement, finance and HR analytics** | AI for invoice anomaly detection, contract analysis, job-description drafting, rota gap forecasting. | NHS Wales Shared Services Partnership (NWSSP) | 2026–2028 |
| 3.4 | **Population health intelligence** | AI-driven PHM cohorts, social-determinants modelling, vaccination uptake prediction. | Public Health Wales | 2027–2029 |
| 3.5 | **Cybersecurity AI** | Behavioural-anomaly detection for IAM, IoMT (medical device) monitoring, ransomware early-warning. | DHCW | 2026–2027 |

## 7. Strategic enablers

### 7.1 Enabler 1 — Infrastructure, data and bilingual AI capability

We cannot deliver the priorities without:

- **A National Data Resource (NDR) at pace.** Continuing DHCW's NDR programme, with a target of a unified, FAIR (Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, Reusable) data platform serving all seven Health Boards by 2028.[^dhcw-ia]
- **Sovereign or appropriately-located compute**. Use of UK-jurisdiction cloud regions (Crown Hosting, NHS-approved hyperscaler regions) for all patient-identifiable data, with on-premises GPU capacity for sensitive training workloads via SAIL Databank and university partners.[^sail]
- **A Welsh-language model layer.** A national capability for Welsh-language fine-tuning and evaluation of foundation models, in partnership with Bangor University, the Welsh Government's Language Technologies Unit and *Cymraeg 2050*.[^cymraeg2050]
- **Standards.** Adoption of FHIR R4+, SNOMED-CT, OpenEHR archetypes; algorithmic transparency standards (DSIT ATRS); model cards and data sheets for every deployed model.[^uk-atrs]

### 7.2 Enabler 2 — Governance, ethics and trust

The most likely cause of failure for an NHS AI strategy is not technology — it is loss of public, clinician or political trust. We will pre-empt this by:

- Establishing the **NHS Wales AI Council** (§10) as the single accountable body.
- Operating a **public NHS Wales AI Register** documenting every deployed clinical AI tool, its intended use, its training population, its evaluation, its bias-audit status and its clinical safety officer.
- Embedding **FUTURE-AI principles** (Fairness, Universality, Traceability, Usability, Robustness, Explainability) in every assurance gate.[^future-ai]
- Adopting the **UK AI Playbook for Government** (ten principles) wholesale, with NHS-Wales addenda for bilingualism and devolved accountability.[^uk-playbook]
- Mandating **independent annual algorithmic audits** for high-risk clinical AI.

### 7.3 Enabler 3 — Workforce, skills and culture

NHS Wales currently lacks the AI-skilled workforce to deliver this strategy at scale. HEIW's 2024 Landscape Review found that *NHS staff felt they lacked adequate training to understand and implement AI technologies, particularly pronounced in clinical disciplines such as radiology and pathology.*[^heiw-landscape]

We will:

- Deliver **AI Literacy 101** to 100% of NHS Wales staff via *Y Tŷ Dysgu* by end-2026 (HEIW partnership);
- Build a **Clinical AI Safety Officer (CAISO)** programme — at least two per Health Board / Trust by 2027;
- Recruit and develop **200+ specialist AI roles** (data scientists, ML engineers, MLOps, AI evaluators) across DHCW and Health Boards by 2028;
- Embed AI-augmented practice in undergraduate and postgraduate curricula via partnerships with Cardiff, Swansea, Bangor, Aberystwyth and the South Wales Medical, Dental and Pharmacy Schools;
- Develop a **bilingual AI fluency** stream so that clinicians can confidently use AI in both languages.

### 7.4 Enabler 4 — Partnership, research and innovation

We will not build everything ourselves. We will:

- Establish a **National Health AI Sandbox** — a shared evaluation environment for vendors, researchers and Health Boards — co-located with SAIL Databank in Swansea.
- Sign a **Four Nations AI Memorandum of Understanding** with NHS England, NHS Scotland and HSC Northern Ireland to share regulatory pathways, clinical evaluations and reusable artefacts.
- Partner with **Welsh universities** through the Health and Care Research Wales infrastructure, the AI for Health and Care Network (AIHCN-Cymru) and the Bevan Commission.
- Engage **patient and public voice** through the Wales Citizens' Jury on Health AI (annual), and through the existing Llais (Citizen Voice Body) infrastructure.[^llais]
- Operate a **transparent vendor framework** through NWSSP, with mandatory adherence to this strategy's standards.

## 8. Welsh language and AI: *Iaith ddigidol*

The Welsh language is not an afterthought; it is a load-bearing pillar of NHS Wales' identity and a statutory obligation under the *Welsh Language (Wales) Measure 2011* and the standards issued thereunder.[^welsh-measure]

Most foundation models today underperform in Welsh. The training corpora are smaller, the named entity recognition is poorer, and clinical terminology in Welsh has historically been under-represented. This creates a genuine equity risk: an AI scribe that hallucinates in Welsh consultations is not merely inconvenient, it is unsafe.

We will:

1. **Mandate bilingual capability at procurement.** From 1 April 2027, no patient-facing or staff-facing AI tool will be commissioned for use in NHS Wales unless it can operate in Welsh and English to a defined Welsh-language quality standard, evaluated by an independent panel.
2. **Build Cymraeg evaluation harnesses.** A standardised Welsh-language clinical NLP benchmark, maintained by DHCW and Bangor University, against which all candidate AI tools must be evaluated.
3. **Invest in Welsh language model capability.** Co-fund a Welsh-language clinical foundation model layer with Welsh Government and the *Cymraeg 2050* programme.[^cymraeg2050]
4. **Recognise *Mwy na geiriau / More than just words*.**[^morethanjustwords] The *active offer* of Welsh-language services applies to AI exactly as it applies to human services.

## 11. Ethical and regulatory framework

### 11.1 The three laws of AI in NHS Wales

Adapted from the GOSH *Three Laws of AI Healthcare*,[^gosh] inspired by the Hippocratic Oath and Asimov's Three Laws:

1. **AI systems must benefit the health and well-being of the individual and prevent harm.** *Primum non nocere* — first do no harm.
2. **AI systems must protect confidentiality and be equitable and unbiased in their healthcare recommendations for all individuals**, including Welsh-language speakers, people from ethnic minorities, rural populations, disabled people, and those facing socioeconomic disadvantage.
3. **AI systems must operate under the supervision of qualified healthcare professionals**, ensuring transparency in their analysis to enable effective oversight. *Meaningful human control at the right stages.*[^uk-playbook]

### 11.2 Adopted principles

We adopt in full:

- The **ten UK AI Playbook principles**;[^uk-playbook]
- **FUTURE-AI**: Fairness, Universality, Traceability, Usability, Robustness, Explainability;[^future-ai]
- The **NHS AI Ethics Initiative** outputs;[^nhse-ki]
- The **MHRA Software and AI as a Medical Device** roadmap;[^mhra-aimd]
- The **NICE Evidence Standards Framework for Digital Health Technologies**;[^nice-esf]
- The **ICO guidance on AI and data protection** and *Explaining decisions made with AI*.[^ico-ai]

### 11.3 Statutory and regulatory landscape

| Instrument | Applies to | NHS Wales action |
|---|---|---|
| Data Protection Act 2018 / UK GDPR | All personal data processing | DPIA mandatory before any AI procurement; ICO consultation for high-risk |
| Common Law Duty of Confidentiality | All patient identifiable data | Section 251 / equivalent Welsh information governance gateway |
| Medical Device Regulations 2002 (UK MDR) | AI as a Medical Device | MHRA registration; clinical investigation where required |
| Welsh Language (Wales) Measure 2011[^welsh-measure] | All Welsh public bodies | Bilingual at procurement (§8) |
| Equality Act 2010 | All NHS Wales services | Equality Impact Assessment (EqIA) for every AI deployment |
| Health and Care Act 2022 (England, but informs NHS England partnerships) | Cross-border AI | Recognition of regulatory equivalence |
| Wellbeing of Future Generations (Wales) Act 2015 | All Welsh public bodies | Sustainable, long-term, integrated thinking applied to AI investment |
| EU AI Act (extra-jurisdictional but influential) | Vendors operating in EU | Vendor assurance includes EU AI Act compliance for high-risk systems[^eu-aiact] |

### 11.4 Assurance pipeline

Every AI use case progresses through six gates (adapted from the One London delivery lifecycle):[^onelondon]

```
1. Idea       → 2. Proof of    → 3. Pilot   → 4. Evaluate → 5. Scale → 6. Monitor
   pipeline      Concept (PoC)     (limited     (TEST           (BAU)     & Maintain
                                   real-world)  framework)
```

Mandatory artefacts at each gate:

- **DPIA + EqIA + Welsh Language Impact Assessment (WLIA)** before PoC;
- **Clinical Safety Case (DCB0129/DCB0160 equivalent under DSPT/NHS Wales standards)** before Pilot;
- **TEST evaluation report** before Scale;
- **Model card, data sheet, ATRS record** before BAU;[^uk-atrs]
- **Continuous monitoring** with drift, bias and safety metrics for the lifetime of the deployment.

## 14. Return on investment (ROI) analysis

> **Methodological note.** All figures below are illustrative analytical estimates extrapolated from the published external evidence cited in §5.2 and applied to publicly-available NHS Wales activity volumes (e.g. ~5 million outpatient appointments per year, ~700,000 hospital admissions per year, ~£11.5bn budget). They are sensitivity-tested at low/central/high cases. They are *not* committed savings and would require validation through standard NHS business-case processes (Five Case Model). They are presented to support strategic prioritisation, not budget setting.

### 14.1 Investment envelope (3 years, 2026–2028)

| Cost line | Year 1 | Year 2 | Year 3 | Total |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| AI infrastructure (compute, storage, MLOps platform) | £8m | £12m | £15m | £35m |

Central case ≈ **0.4–0.5% of NHS Wales annual budget**, recoverable within Year 2.

| Metric | Low | Central | High |
|---|---|---|---|
| 3-year benefit | £200m | £320m | £480m |

Beyond purely financial benefit, the strategy is expected to deliver substantial *unmonetised* benefit in:

- Reduction in clinician burnout and improved retention;
- Public confidence in NHS Wales as a digital-first health service;
- Strengthened position of the Welsh language in healthcare;
- Research income, IP and inward investment.

### 14.4 What would falsify this case

We will explicitly track the following falsification signals; if observed, we will pause expansion and re-plan:

- AI-related serious incidents > 3 per year that would not have occurred without AI;
- Patient trust in AI < 50% on the annual citizen survey;
- Clinician confidence falling year-on-year;
- Realised benefit < 50% of central case at Year 1 review;
- Material bilingual safety differential (Welsh-language users experiencing worse outcomes from AI tools).

## 17. Risk management

Adapted from GOSH Appendix 8 and tailored to NHS Wales' devolved context.[^gosh]

| Risk category | Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Bias and fairness** | AI underperforms for Welsh speakers, ethnic minorities, rural patients, older patients | High | High | Bilingual evaluation harness; mandatory EqIA + WLIA; independent annual bias audit; diverse training data; Llais oversight |
| **Clinical safety** | AI misdiagnosis / inappropriate recommendation | Medium | Severe | Clinical Safety Officer per LHB; human-in-the-loop mandatory; clinical safety case (DCB0129/0160-equivalent); incident reporting via Datix-equivalent; MHRA Yellow Card[^nhse-ai] |
| **Automation bias** | Clinicians defer to AI without critical evaluation | High | High | Mandatory training module on automation bias; UI must make uncertainty visible; "explain why" required; periodic deliberate "AI off" audits |
| **Data privacy** | Unauthorised access; data leakage to vendor | Medium | Severe | UK-jurisdiction processing only; DPIA mandatory; encryption end-to-end; supplier contracts with audit rights |
| **Cybersecurity** | AI-specific attacks (prompt injection, model poisoning, data exfiltration) | Medium | High | Threat modelling per system; AI red-teaming; behavioural anomaly detection; alignment with NCSC AI principles |
| **Welsh language equity** | AI tools rolled out English-only by default | High | High | Bilingual mandate at procurement (§8); WLIA at every gate; Welsh Language Commissioner liaison |
| **Model drift** | Model performance degrades over time | High | Medium | Continuous monitoring; quarterly performance reviews; pre-defined re-training triggers; archive of training data |
| **Vendor lock-in** | NHS Wales becomes dependent on a small number of vendors | Medium | High | Multi-supplier framework; open standards (FHIR, HL7); reusable evaluation harnesses; right to model card and data sheet |
| **Operational integration** | AI adds work rather than removing it | Medium | High | Co-design with clinicians; time-and-motion studies before/after; clear "stop deployment" criteria |
| **Public trust** | Single high-profile failure undermines confidence | Medium | Severe | Radical transparency via AI Register; active public engagement; clear redress pathways; pre-emptive communications |
| **Financial overrun** | Costs exceed business case | High | Medium | Stage-gated investment; benefit realisation tracked quarterly; portfolio approach |
| **Workforce resistance** | Staff fear job loss, reject deployment | Medium | High | Trade union partnership from day 1; clear "augment not replace" messaging; redeployment-not-redundancy compact |
| **Sustainability** | AI compute drives energy use up | High | Medium | Carbon intensity per inference KPI; preference for efficient models; sovereign green compute |
| **Devolution friction** | Regulatory or commercial divergence with England limits cross-border partnership | Medium | Medium | Four Nations AI MoU; recognised regulatory equivalence; joint procurement where beneficial |
